Key information for Cambridgeshire, school layer, LSOA geography

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) schools layer for Cambridgeshire. The data were generated on 2018-01-24 and this document was created on 2018-01-24. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. The PCT source code can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This layer of the PCT uses origin-destination data on travel to school from the 2011 National School Census (NSC). The interface is currently displaying the number of children travelling by different modes to school from the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) where they live (LSOAs have an average size 360 children).

There were 108,531 school children living in Cambridgeshire recorded in the 2011 NSC. All of these children are included in the zones data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download. There were 108,332 school children attending a school in Cambridgeshire recorded in the 2011 NSC. All of these children are included in the destinations data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download.

Finally, the Route Network (LSOA) is built from all origin-destination fast routes that have have a fast route distance <5km (for primary school children)/<10km (for secondary school children).

For more details of methods please see User Manual xx.


The Cambridgeshire region

Figure 1 shows a map of Cambridgeshire, including the regional boundary (thick black border), local authority boundaries (blue lines) and LSOA zones in the region (grey with a black border).

Figure 1: Map of the Cambridgeshire region The left-hand map in Figure 2 shows the proportion of children in each zone with a fast route distance to school less than 5km. The average proportion was 83% across zones in Cambridgeshire, compared with a national average of 85%. The right-hand map shows the average hilliness of the fastest routes used for travelling to school in each zone. The average was 0.9% across zones in Cambridgeshire, compared with a national average of 2.2%.

Cycling levels in each scenario for Cambridgeshire

At baseline (NSC 2011), the proportion of commuters who cycled to school in Cambridgeshire was 8.7%, compared to the national average of 1.8% in England as a whole. The percentage of cycling expected was 2.5%, based on the distance and hilliness of school trips in the region using the baseline model for England.
