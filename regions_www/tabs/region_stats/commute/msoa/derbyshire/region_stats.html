Key information for Derbyshire, commute layer, MSOA geography

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) commuting layer for Derbyshire. The data were generated on 2020-05-25 and this document was created on 2020-10-21. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. The PCT source code can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This layer of the PCT uses origin-destination data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The interface is currently displaying the number of commuters travelling by different modes between Middle Super Output Area zones (MSOAs, average size 3,325 commuters). There were 439,614 commuters living in Derbyshire recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these commuters are included in the zones data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download.

Adding in inter-regional flows, there were 490,855 commuters living or working in Derbyshire in the 2011 Census. All these commuters are included in the All flows files on the data download pages.

Straight lines, Fast routes and Quieter routes are both visualised on the map interface and available to download. In the downloads, these lines and routes contain all 4,520 flows between MSOAs that a) start or end in Derbyshire, b) have a fast route distance <20km, and c) contain at least 10 commuters (by any mode, in either direction). These 4,520 between-zone MSOA flows contain 293,287 commuters who either live or work in Derbyshire. To speed up load times, the online map only shows those line and routes with a fast route distance <10km.

Between-zone MSOA flows exclude within-MSOA travel, when the MSOA zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-MSOA travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 46,881 commuters living in Derbyshire. Between-zone flows also exclude those with no fixed place of work.

Finally, the Route Network (LSOA, clickable) and the Route Network (LSOA, image) are built from all between-zone LSOA fast routes (including any that start or end in Derbyshire). The online version of the clickable route network and the downloads both include all route segments. For more details please see User Manual C1.

See Lovelace et al. (2017) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and the methods used to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

Figure 1: Map of the Derbyshire region The left-hand map in Figure 2 shows the proportion of commuters in each zone with a fast route commute distance less than 10km (calculated excluding people with no fixed workplace). Cycling levels in each scenario for Derbyshire

At baseline (2011 Census), the proportion of commuters who cycled to work in Derbyshire was 2.2%, compared to the national average of 3.1% in England and Wales as a whole. The percentage of cycling expected was 2.3%, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England and Wales.

Table 1 summarises the proportion of commuters cycling, walking, driving and using other modes under each scenario in Derbyshire, based on aggregating across all residents in all the MSOA zones.

Health and carbon impacts in Derbyshire

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.

Table 2 illustrates the health and carbon impacts of the different scenarios in Derbyshire, based on aggregating across all residents in all the MSOA zones. For each scenario, comparisons are made with a counterfactual in which nobody in the region cycled ('No cyclists'), and with the observed level of cycling in the 2011 Census.
