Key information for Wales, commute layer, MSOA geography

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) commuting layer for Wales. The data were generated on 2019-01-10 and this document was created on 2019-01-10. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. The PCT source code can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This layer of the PCT uses origin-destination data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The interface is currently displaying the number of commuters travelling by different modes between Middle Super Output Area zones (MSOAs, average size 3,325 commuters). There were 1,226,591 commuters living in Wales recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these commuters are included in the zones data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download.

Adding in inter-regional flows, there were 1,243,467 commuters living or working in Wales in the 2011 Census. All these commuters are included in the All flows files on the data download pages.

Straight lines, Fast routes and Quieter routes are both visualised on the map interface and available to download. These lines and routes contain all 6,725 flows between MSOAs that a) start or end in Wales, b) have a fast route distance <20km, and c) contain at least 10 commuters (by any mode, in either direction). These 6,725 between-zone MSOA flows contain 668,812 commuters who either live or work in Wales.

Between-zone MSOA flows exclude within-MSOA travel, when the MSOA zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-MSOA travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 169,288 commuters living in Wales. Between-zone flows also exclude those with no fixed place of work.

The Route Network (MSOA) is built from all between-zone MSOA fast routes that a) start and end in Wales, b) have a fast route distance <20km, and c) contain at least 10 commuters (by any mode, in either direction). It includes 647,890 commuters who live and work in Wales. The Lower Super Output Area Route Network (LSOA) is built from all between-zone LSOA fast routes that a) start or end in Wales, and b) have a fast route distance <20km. For more details please see User Manual C.

See Lovelace et al. (2017) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and the methods used to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

Figure 1: Map of Wales The left-hand map in Figure 2 shows the proportion of commuters in each zone with a fast route commute distance less than 10km (calculated excluding people with no fixed workplace). Cycling levels in each scenario for Wales

At baseline (2011 Census), the proportion of commuters who cycled to work in Wales was 1.6%, compared to the national average of 3.1% in England and Wales as a whole. The percentage of cycling expected was 2.1%, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England and Wales.

Table 1 summarises the proportion of commuters cycling, walking, driving and using other modes under each scenario in Wales, based on aggregating across all residents in all the MSOA zones.

Health and carbon impacts in Wales

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.

Table 2 illustrates the health and carbon impacts of the different scenarios in Wales, based on aggregating across all residents in all the MSOA zones.
