Key information for Cornwall and Isles of Scilly, commute layer, LSOA geography

This document provides information about the data underlying the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT) commuting layer for Cornwall and Isles of Scilly. The data were generated on 2020-05-25 and this document was created on 2020-10-21. The PCT is an open source tool for sustainable transport planning, released under the conditions of the Affero GPL. The PCT source code can be modified by others as long as attribution is made to the original.

This layer of the PCT uses origin-destination data on travel to work from the 2011 Census. The interface is currently displaying the number of commuters travelling by different modes between Lower Super Output Area zones (LSOAs, average size 690 commuters). There were 207,820 commuters living in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly recorded in the 2011 Census. All of these commuters are included in the zones data that are visualised on the map interface and available for download.

Adding in inter-regional flows, there were 210,932 commuters living or working in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly in the 2011 Census, and commuting in a flow containing at least 3 commuters (in either direction). These commuters are included in the All flows files on the data download pages. Flows containing fewer than 3 commuters are excluded from the visualisation and download files for data protection reasons.

Straight lines, Fast routes and Quieter routes are both visualised on the map interface and available to download. In the downloads, these lines and routes contain all 5,766 flows between LSOAs that a) start or end in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly, b) have a fast route distance <10km, and c) contain at least 5 commuters (by any mode, in either direction). These 5,766 between-zone LSOA flows contain 115,016 commuters who either live or work in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly. To speed up load times, the online map only shows those lines and routes with a fast route distance <10km.

Between-zone LSOA flows exclude within-LSOA travel, when the LSOA zone of origin is the same as the zone of destination. Within-LSOA travel is represented by red points on the map when the lines are shown, and accounts for 15,888 commuters living in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly. Between-zone flows also exclude those with no fixed place of work.

Finally, the Route Network (LSOA, clickable) and the Route Network (LSOA, image) are built from all between-zone LSOA fast routes (including any that start or end in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly). The online version of the clickable route network and the downloads both include all route segments. For more details please see User Manual C1.

See Lovelace et al. (2017) for details of the methods used to estimate the cycling, health and carbon impacts of each scenario, and the methods used to visualise results at the area, line, route and route network level.

Cycling levels in each scenario for Cornwall and Isles of Scilly

At baseline (2011 Census), the proportion of commuters who cycled to work in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly was 1.9%, compared to the national average of 3.1% in England and Wales as a whole. The percentage of cycling expected was 1.3%, based on the distance and hilliness of commutes in the region using the baseline model for England and Wales.

Health and carbon impacts in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly

A modified version of the 2014 HEAT tool was used to calculate health impacts, based on the extra physical activity gained through new cycling trips in each scenario, minus the physical activity displaced in any former walking trips that have now been switched to cycling. CO2 impacts are derived from changes in distance driven per scenario.

Table 2 illustrates the health and carbon impacts of the different scenarios in Cornwall and Isles of Scilly, based on aggregating across all residents in all the LSOA zones.
